Reproductive System and Birth, growth and Aging.

1) the reproductive system is divided into two parts sperm and egg.
Male anatomy: sperm mixed with fluid which is secreted by several glands. the seminal vesicles are pair of glands that secrete a fluid into the vas deferens, which produce a thick fluid that is rich in fructose (fructose is what provides energy for sperm cells). prostate glands are glands that produce fluid that helps sperm move.
Female anatomy: Eggs are female sex cells. female reproductive system has three main functions 1) first is to produce eggs. 2) second is o receive sperm. 3) third is to provide an environment in which a fertilized egg can develop.

2) birth, growth, and aging: birth has three main recognizable stages. 1) dilation. 2) expulsion. 3) placental the dilation stage is when a female goes through to give birth (this is called labor). as labor continues contraction happens. expulsion occurs when the baby is pushed out of the mother's body by involuntary uterine contractions. the last stage is called the placental stage, it appears 10 to 15 minutes after the baby is born, and the placenta separates from the wall of the uterus. the placenta is pushed out of the body, along with whatever is left of the embryonic membranes. growth and aging happen after the baby is born. there 3 stages of growth, 1) infancy 2) childhood 3) adolescence adulthood
