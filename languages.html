Novel Reading



John Boyne's 2006 cathartic novel, The Boy in Striped Pyjamas, is a work of holocaust fiction. The story takes place during World War II,
when Hitler—also known as the Fuehrer or Fury—believed that Jews were inferior to other humans and went on to establish concentration
camps across Europe. Despite being a fictional novel, the story is based on actual occurrences that took place during the Holocaust.
The book is written from the point of view of Bruno, a nine-year-old. The main protagonist of the novel is Bruno, who resides with his
parents and older sister Gretel. His innocent, naive views are very touching! He was compelled to relocate from Berlin to Auschwitz
due to his father's job. He is a curious, impressionable young guy who loves to explore new places and go one adventures

However, he prefers Berlin than “Out-With” (Auschwitz). He glances out of his window and sees something appealing on the other side of
the fence. He comes across groups of people dressed in blue and white striped pajamas. He visits the fence as the one who enjoys
exploration and meets Shmuel, a Jewish boy. Despite the fence dividing them, they form an illicit friendship. Shmuel describes his
miserable life on the other side of the fence, including how he lost his father during their extermination.

Before returning to Berlin, to assist his friend Shmuel in his search for his father, Bruno makes a commitment to cross the border once.
Bruno is unaware that his father serves as the commandant and is an employee of the "Fury." Bruno meets a variety of personalities along
the way, including Lieutenant Kotler, Pavel, Maria, his social studies instructor, etc., and learns bits and pieces of what's happening.
The remainder of the novel focuses on Bruno and Shmuel's deep bond and their search for his father. This novel is worthwhile to read even though it has a tragic conclusion. It's an effortless novel with profound feelings and significance.



It's definitely worth the read!
