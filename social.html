The history of Telangana bega in 800to 500 BCE also known as the rule of Ashoka and Chalukya. The rulers subsequently changed and passed to Sarhavaahanas, Kakatiyas and many mroe.

Telangana experienced its golden age during the rule of Sathavaahanas and Kakatiyas. They have ruled most past of southern india which includes Telangana, Andhrapradesh and some part of karnataka.

Kakatiyas

The Kakatiyas ruled from 1083 to 1323 AD. Rani Rudhrama Devi and Prataparudra 11 were the most prominent rulers of the Kakatiyas.

The Kakatiyas were weakend in 1309 by with the attack of Malik Kafur and was dissolved in 1232 by the attack of Mohammed Bin Thuglug. Soon later, Bahami Sultanate, Qutb Shahi and Asaf Hahi have ruled. The reign was in control of Delhi Sultanate till 1687 when Golconda fell into the hands of Aurangazeb.

    • Purani Haveli, Hyderabad

    Purani Haveli also known as Masarrat Mahal palace is a palace located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It was the official residence of the Nizam. It was also known as Haveli Khadeem, which means old mansion, was constructed for Sikander Jah, Asaf Jah III by his father Ali Khan Bahadur, Asaf Jah II.

    Golconda Fort

    Golconda is a historic fortess located near the outskirts of Hyderabad. This fort was built in the 10th century by Prataparudra using mud, stone and clay walls. It was originally called Mankal. Golconda was the principal capital of the Qutub Shahi kings. The inner fort contains ruins of palaces, mosques and a hill top pavilion, which rises about 130 meters high and gives a beautiful view of different buildings.

    Chowmahalla Palace

    Chowmahalla Palace was bult in 1750 by the Asaf Jahi dynasty. This palace over the decades a synthesis of many architectural styles and influences emerged. The palace consists of two courtyards as well as the grand Khilwat (the Dharbar Hall), fountains and gardens.
